CROWSNEST CASTLE – SUSPECT DOSSIER
This document lists all known suspects and staff connected to the Crowsnest Castle murder.
Intended for review in the Library / Detective’s Office.

PRIMARY SUSPECTS


Lord Edmund Crowsnest
Role: Owner of Crowsnest Castle
Possible Motive: Financial pressure, hidden debts uncovered by the victim
Notes: Had authority over everyone in the castle


Lady Margaret Crowsnest
Role: Lady of the house
Possible Motive: Inheritance, strained marriage
Notes: Jewelry and personal items may link her to the crime


Sebastian Hale
Role: Family solicitor
Possible Motive: Will revisions shortly before the murder
Notes: Legal knowledge, access to private documents


Clara Ravenswood
Role: Niece and expected heir
Possible Motive: Would inherit the estate
Notes: Timeline conflicts, secret meetings


Dr. Elias Morton
Role: Family physician
Possible Motive: Concealed medical information
Notes: Medical inconsistencies around time of death


Victor Blackwell
Role: Business partner
Possible Motive: Failing partnership
Notes: Witnessed arguing with the victim


Agnes Whitlock
Role: Castle archivist
Possible Motive: Knew long-hidden family secrets
Notes: Controls access to old records


Thomas Greene
Role: Son of former groundskeeper
Possible Motive: Family grievances against the estate
Notes: Familiar with hidden access routes


Isabelle Fournier
Role: Visiting art dealer
Possible Motive: Blackmail involving forged art
Notes: Had no legitimate reason to possess certain items


Julian Cross
Role: Private secretary
Possible Motive: Schedule and access manipulation
Notes: Controlled who saw the victim and when



CASTLE STAFF (NOT PRIMARY SUSPECTS)


Harold Finch
Role: Head butler
Key Knowledge: Who was served and at what times


Miriam Locke
Role: Housekeeper
Key Knowledge: Found disturbances before CSI arrived


Edgar Bell
Role: Night watchman
Key Knowledge: Saw someone leaving the Ballroom late at night



NARRATIVE NOTES
The body was removed by investigators.
What remains are photographs, timelines, and contradictions.
Evidence places people in locations; it does not prove guilt alone.
Document Status: Working dossier – subject to updates.
